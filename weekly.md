# Weekly Plan 

## Weekdays (2.5 hrs/day x 5 = 12.5 hrs)

### Monday – Codeforces Focus
- 1 hr: Virtual contest or 3 mid-difficulty problems (1500–1700)
- 1 hr: Upsolve 1–2 failed problems from recent contest
- 0.5 hr: Read 1 advanced editorial + write key insight in journal

### Tuesday – Math Day
- 1 hr: Learn 1 new math topic (see math list)
- 1 hr: Solve 2–3 math-heavy problems (Project Euler, CF math tags)
- 0.5 hr: Write 1 short proof or explanation

### Wednesday – Puzzle + Interview Day
- 1.5 hrs: Logic or math puzzle (solve + reflect)
- 1 hr: Leetcode Hard or CTCI-style whiteboard problem

### Thursday – C++/Systems Focus
- 1.5 hrs: Read or implement advanced C++ concept (from list)
- 1 hr: Code small benchmark or perf test to understand behavior

### Friday – Light Review + Reflection
- 1 hr: Flashcard/notes review of week (math, C++, puzzles)
- 1 hr: One tough problem or mock question
- 0.5 hr: Resume/portfolio/log update

---

## Weekend (10–12 hrs total)

### Saturday (4 hrs)
- 2 hrs: Virtual Codeforces or AtCoder contest
- 1.5 hrs: Upsolve + read editorials
- 0.5 hr: Puzzle or math brain teaser

### Sunday (6–8 hrs)
- 2 hrs: Math + probability drills (see math list)
- 1.5 hrs: Puzzle or whiteboard-style mock
- 1.5 hrs: Advanced C++ concept or project (lock-free, perf, cache sim)
- Optional: Read Real World OCaml or blog post

## Weekly Goals
- Codeforces: 10+ problems solved
- Math: 1 new topic learned + drilled
- Puzzle: 1–2 solved
- C++: 1 new deep concept internalized
- Mock: 1 timed whiteboard session
